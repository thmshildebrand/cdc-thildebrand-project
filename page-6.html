Cafe de Casa is a family Brazilian restaurant owned by Lucimar Canedo and her two daughters, Thais and Amanda Moreira. The family took over the restaurant in 2011 and today it has two additional locations, one in Fisherman’s Wharf and the newest one in the Castro. Canedo moved to San Francisco em 2004 from a small town in the countryside of Brazil and started working as a house cleaner and as a cook at the pizzeria her friend’s husband owned in the Richmond District. Eventually, Canedo moved her two daughters to the United States to help with her food enterprise and to seek a better life for the three of them.

She ended her work at the pizzeria and began selling Brazilian food wholesale to cafes like Cafe de Casa in South San Francisco, which was then owned by someone else. Their transition into restaurateurs started with a debt issue: The then-owner of Cafe de Casa owed Canedo more than six months worth of pastries. Canedo spotted an opportunity and approached him with an agreement in which she would take over the business, renting and operating the cafe at a discounted rate in lieu of the debt payments. By the end of 2010, she and her daughters took it over and have operated it ever since.
